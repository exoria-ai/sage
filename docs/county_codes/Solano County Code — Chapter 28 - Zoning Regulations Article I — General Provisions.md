## <a id="sec-28-01"></a>28.01 — Definitions

For the purposes of this Chapter, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them by this Section:

### A (excerpted definitions in provided snippet)

- **Accessory building.** A subordinate building or structure located on the same lot, the use of which is customarily incidental to that of the main building or to the principal use of the land.

  - **Agricultural accessory building.** A building, greenhouse, or other structure utilized in conjunction with commercial crop production or grazing, including storage of agricultural products/supplies/equipment used in agricultural operations. Excludes dwellings, residential accessory buildings, employee housing, stables, dairies/other confined animal facilities, and agricultural processing facilities.

  - **Residential accessory building.** A detached building accessory to a single-family dwelling (e.g., detached garage, storage shed, guest house, pool house). In any R District, an accessory building (other than a greenhouse) on a lot with a dwelling is classified as residential accessory building even if used with crop production/grazing. Excludes a secondary dwelling or accessory dwelling unit.

- **Accessory use.** A subordinate use customarily incidental to that of the main building or principal use of the land and located on the same lot.

- **Agricultural education.** Information/knowledge/skills pertaining to agriculture conducted concurrently with crop production and accessory to the primary agricultural use (e.g., demonstration gardens, interactive seminars).

  - **Agricultural education, minor facility.** Occurs on an existing farm; no new permanent buildings/facilities; does not require a Building Permit for change of occupancy; may use temporary structures (portable sanitation, temporary food service).

  - **Agricultural education, major facility.** Requires construction of new buildings/facilities, or requires a Building Permit for change of occupancy of existing buildings/structures.

- **Agricultural homestay.** A dwelling on a farm (ag products are resident family’s primary income) offering two or more guest rooms for transient occupancy, with or without meals.

- **Agricultural processing facility.** A fixed establishment processing/packaging crops after harvest to prepare for market (examples listed in snippet include drying, sorting/grading/packing, canning/freezing/slicing/juicing/preserving, nut hulling/shelling, and *breweries/distilleries/alcohol fuel production*). Includes accessory uses like offices/labs, tasting facilities with retail sales of agricultural products (processed on premises or off site by operator), promotional/educational items, and tours. Excludes wineries, slaughterhouses, and other separately defined/regulated processing operations; excludes sorting/packing within a permitted roadside stand; excludes mobile field-processing without permanent buildings.

  - **Small:** < 20,000 sq ft covered+uncovered processing area; **no** tasting facilities or retail sales.  
  - **Medium:** < 40,000 sq ft covered+uncovered processing area (and not “small”).  
  - **Large:** > 40,000 sq ft covered+uncovered processing area, **or** a brewery/distillery of any size.

- **Agricultural Recycling and Composting.** Composting/recycling of agricultural organic materials (on-site or off-site) for beneficial off-site agricultural use.

- **Agricultural research facility.** Study/testing/design/analysis/experimental development/demonstration/teaching/training related to commercial agriculture (includes research/demonstration of processing equipment/methods/operations) but not primarily commercial production.

  - **Small:** < 20,000 sq ft buildings/structures  
  - **Medium:** 20,000–40,000 sq ft buildings/structures  
  - **Large:** > 40,000 sq ft buildings/structures

- **Agricultural trucking services and facilities.** Trucking/hauling where > 50% of total gross income in a 12-month period is from hauling unfinished agricultural products for processing.

  - **Small:** ≤ 5 trucks and operator resides on property  
  - **Medium:** 6–10 trucks and operator resides on property  
  - **Large:** > 11 trucks, **or** any number where operator does not reside on property

- **Agricultural Warehousing and Storage.** Storage of agricultural products to be sold/distributed by someone other than the producer; no retail sales on premises.

- **Agriculture.** Cultivating/harvesting/rearing/managing livestock; farming/horticulture/forestry; production of plants/animals useful to humans.

- **Agritourism.** Commercial activities at a working farm/ranch (or other agriculturally zoned property as permitted) for visitor enjoyment (recreation, education/tours, direct sales, lodging, entertainment). Excludes an amusement facility as defined.

- **Agritourism event.** Temporary, public, ≤ one season, no new permanent structures; consecutive/recurrent use of same parcel regulated as agritourism facility or other listed use.

- **Agritourism Facility.** Agritourism enterprise (not specifically listed in applicable allowed-use table) on a working farm/ranch by owner; incidental/compatible with commercial ag operations.

- **Alley.** Secondary means of vehicular access; not intended for general traffic circulation.

- **Amusement Facilities.** Examples listed include bowling alley, billiard parlor, dance hall, skating rink, athletic club/gym, pool, theater, shooting gallery; coin-operated devices; auditorium/exhibition hall/sports arena; miniature golf, amusement rides, indoor court games, drive-in theater; model vehicle courses; similar uses.

- **Animal Hospital or Veterinary Clinic.** Diagnosis/treatment/prevention where animals not boarded overnight except as medically necessary.

  - **Large animal:** primarily for large animals.

- **Animal, small.** Chicken, turkey, rabbit, duck, mink, hamster, chinchilla, pot belly pig, or similar.

- **Animal units.** Sum total units assigned per animal per table in snippet:

  | Type of Animal | Units per Animal |
  |---|---|
  | Mature cattle, horses, or similar size | 1.00 |
  | Yearling cattle, horses, or similar size | 0.75 |
  | Calves, colts, or similar size | 0.40 |
  | Hogs > 90 days old | 1.00 |
  | Two or more litters of hogs | 0.50 per hog |
  | One litter of hogs | None |
  | Mature sheep, goats, or similar size | 0.20 |
  | Lambs, kids, or similar size | 0.15 |

- **Aquaculture.** Propagation/cultivation/maintenance/harvesting of aquatic plants/animals (marine/brackish/fresh); excludes aquariums/ponds for pets/landscaping.

  - **Small:** < 10 acres  
  - **Medium:** 10–50 acres  
  - **Large:** > 50 acres

- **Automobile parking lot.** Operable, duly licensed autos parked by individual owners for ≤ 72 hours.

- **Battery energy storage system (BESS).** Electrochemical device rated capacity ≥ 1,000 kWh (1 MWh) that charges/collects energy, stores, and discharges later for electricity/grid services.

- **Bed and Breakfasts Inn.** Dwelling occupied by a resident family offering two or more guest rooms for transient occupancy, with or without meals.

- **Block.** Property on one side of a street between nearest intersecting/intercepting streets (or street and railroad ROW/unsubdivided acreage/watercourse/body of water).

- **Building.** Any structure used/intended for supporting or sheltering any use/occupancy.

- **Building, accessory.** A subordinate building on the same lot customarily incidental to main building/principal land use.

- **Building, height.** Average height above finished grade of highest part of building.

- **Building, main.** Building conducting the principal use on the building site; in residential districts, the primary dwelling is a main building.

- **Building site.** Ground area of building(s), excluding street, with required open space and principal frontage on a street.

- **Camp grounds.** Land used/let/rented for campers traveling by automobile or otherwise, or temporary tents/similar quarters.

- **Cattery.** Premises where 12+ cats are kept/harbored/maintained.

  - **Small:** 12–24 cats  
  - **Large:** > 24 cats

- **Certified Farmers Market.** Approved by County agricultural commissioner; certified farmers sell only certified products they grow.

  - **Small:** ≤ 9 vendors  
  - **Medium:** 10–20 vendors  
  - **Large:** > 20 vendors

- **Clinic, medical and dental.** Outpatient examination/treatment by staff of doctors with assistants/equipment.

- **Club.** Clubs except those whose chief activity is a service customarily carried on as a business.

- **Commercial coach.** Vehicle designed/equipped for human occupancy for industrial/professional/commercial purposes.

- **Commercial Auctions and Agricultural Equipment Sales.** Third-party auction/sales storing/refurbishing and selling used farming equipment/implements/vehicles/machinery (excluding passenger vehicles or ≤1-ton pickups).

- **Commercial cannabis activities.** Cannabis uses requiring State license or otherwise regulated by the State (testing lab, distributor, transporter, retailers, microbusiness, collective/cooperative, cultivator/nursery, manufacturer/processor, etc.) per MAUCRSA (B&P Code 26000–26231.2, as amended). Delivery allowed to any zoning district within Solano County if licensee has delivery licenses. Excludes temporary cannabis events (CCR Title 16, Div 42, Ch 5, §§5601–5603 as amended). Excludes personal and primary caregiver cultivation defined in §28.82.

- **Commercial Kitchen, Agricultural.** Permanent food facility (per CA Uniform Retail Food Facilities Law) on a farm, not associated with an eating establishment/catering/mobile vendor; for storage/preparation/handling food for sale directly to consumer; may be attached to a roadside stand.

- **Commercial Outdoor Recreation.** Outdoor recreation uses (parks, fields, golf, etc.) and similar as determined by Planning Commission.

- **Commercial Solar Energy Facility.** Solar energy conversion system converting solar to utility power for primary purpose of resale/off-site use.

- **Community care facility.** Facility (not County-owned/operated) providing residential care or day care to persons needing services/supervision/assistance; may provide incidental medical services. Residential/day care serving 6 or fewer persons (excluding licensee/family/staff) treated as a residential use; family day care home licensed under CA Child Day Care Facilities Act treated as residential use.

- **Companion living unit.** Temporary mobilehome subordinate/detached from principal residence providing independent living quarters for adult persons 60+ or handicapped/convalescent; either principal residence or unit must be owner-occupied.

- **Complementary Commercial Facility.** Small commercial facility supporting marsh oriented recreation (bait shop, small boat rental, refreshment stand).

- **Confined Animal Facility (CAF).** Dairy/stockyard/feedlot/similar where animals confined and surface becomes bare of feed growth in normal growing season; excludes normal grazing, supplemental feeding areas/corrals/working facilities temporarily confined incidental to grazing. Classified Small/Medium/Large per max animals; below Small threshold not included. Threshold table in snippet:

  | Animal Sector | Small CAF | Medium CAF | Large CAF |
  |---|---:|---:|---:|
  | Cattle or cow/calf | 20–299 | 300–999 | 1,000+ |
  | Mature dairy cow | 14–199 | 200–699 | 700+ |
  | Veal calves | 20–299 | 300–999 | 1,000+ |
  | Horses | 10–149 | 150–499 | 500+ |
  | Sheep, lambs or goats | 198–2,999 | 3,000–9,999 | 10,000+ |

- **Conservation bank.** Land managed for natural resource values with credits sold for mitigation; includes mitigation bank; excludes conservation easement without credit sales and with continued/allowed agricultural use.

- **Corporation yard.** Storage of construction materials/machinery used by operator in their business.

- **Cottage Industry.** Home-based business with limited goods/services or limited manufacturing; accessory to and conducted primarily by resident family.

- **County boundary.** Boundary of County or any incorporated municipality within County.

- **Courts.** Open unoccupied space (not a yard) on same lot bounded on 2+ sides by buildings.

- **Crop production.** Commercial production of crops (grains/vegetables/fruits/grapes/nuts/flowers/seeds/ornamentals/tree/sod farms etc.) plus associated services/harvesting; includes in-field processing/sales not involving permanent structure.

- **Crowing Fowl.** Male fowl (roosters, drakes, turkeys, peacocks/pea fowl).

- **Custom Farm Services.** Provision of custom farm services (hay baling, irrigation services, land leveling, water development/services, farm labor contractors, etc.).

### Dwelling-related definitions (from snippet)
- **Dwelling.** Single residential unit with complete independent living facilities for one family (living/sleeping/eating/cooking/sanitation).

  - **Accessory dwelling unit (ADU).** Attached/detached ADU on same lot in residential zoning district; includes repurposed existing space; accessory to single-family or multifamily dwelling.

  - **Duplex.** Multifamily dwelling with two units of approx equal area separated by common wall/floor; ADU may also be attached/within as provided.

  - **Dwelling group.** 2+ detached/semidetached dwellings on one ownership with common yard/court; excludes SF or MF with detached ADU; excludes primary with detached secondary dwelling.

  - **Dwelling unit.** Individual dwelling (not ADU/JADU) within MF or mixed occupancy building.

  - **Efficiency dwelling unit.** Only one habitable room.

  - **Junior accessory dwelling unit (JADU).** ADU ≤ 500 sq ft, fully within existing/proposed SF dwelling; may have separate or shared sanitation.

  - **Manufactured dwelling.** Certified under federal manufactured housing standards; installed on foundation as fixture unless temporary or in a mobile home park.

  - **Multifamily dwelling.** 2+ units (apartment houses/flats).

  - **Primary dwelling.** If two SF dwellings on lot (excluding employee housing): first built is primary unless later-built is larger (then larger is primary).

  - **Secondary dwelling.** Independent unit on same parcel as primary; detached/attached/within; not counted as additional unit for density calculations under zoning/General Plan.

  - **Single-family dwelling.** Detached building (not detached ADU) meeting County building regs for one family; includes manufactured dwelling; excludes tent/RV.

  - **Temporary dwelling.** RV or manufactured dwelling installed as chattel, used for limited fixed term.

- **Emergency Shelter.** One or more dwelling units otherwise allowed, or converted commercial/industrial building otherwise allowed, operated under State Emergency Housing and Assistance Program (HSC §50800 et seq.) with minimal supportive services.

## <a id="sec-28-02"></a>28.02 — Purpose of Zoning Plan

A zoning plan is adopted to provide a precise plan for residential, commercial, industrial, agricultural, public, and other land uses in the County in order to:

- **A.** Protect the established character and social and economic values of agricultural, residential, commercial, industrial, recreational, and other areas within the County which have developed in a healthy and orderly manner;
- **B.** Encourage beneficial development of those areas which have grown with conflicting or uneconomic patterns of use; and
- **C.** Assist in providing a definite and publicly approved plan of development to guide, control and stimulate the future growth of the County in accordance with the need of the County and in proper relation to other land use areas in the region.

## <a id="sec-28-03"></a>28.03 — Interpretation of Chapter

### A. General Rules
1. Provisions of this chapter are **minimum requirements**, except where expressly stated as maximum.
2. “**Shall**,” “**will**,” “**is to**,” and “**are to**” are mandatory.  
   “**Should**” is not mandatory but must be fulfilled **or** applicant must demonstrate an alternative fulfilling the intent.  
   “**May**” is permissive.

### B. Unlisted Land Uses
If a proposed land use is not described/identified, the **Zoning Administrator** may determine the use is substantially similar to a described/identified use, and process the application as functionally equivalent. If the similar use requires a discretionary approval (e.g., use permit), the Planning Commission is not bound by the Zoning Administrator’s determination.

### C. Aliquot Divisions of an Irregular Section of Land
Land divisions consistent with Subdivision Map Act and Chapter 26 (Subdivisions) must meet minimum parcel size per zoning district (e.g., A-20, A-40, A-80, A-160, R-R-10, R-R-5, etc.).

For minimum lot area compliance: PLSS surveyed sections containing less than a full aliquot part of a standard 640-acre section are deemed equivalent to a full aliquot part **if** legal description describes the aliquot part and deviation is < 10%. Resultant parcels must be equal size.

Aliquot parts table:

| Aliquot Part | Total Area |
|---|---:|
| Full section | 640 acres |
| One-half section | 320 acres |
| One-quarter section | 160 acres |
| One-eighth (quarter-quarter) | 80 acres |
| One-sixteenth | 40 acres |
| One-thirty-second | 20 acres |
| One-sixty-fourth | 10 acres |
| One-one-hundred twenty-eighth | 5 acres |

## <a id="sec-28-04"></a>28.04 — Public Notice Requirements

When a public hearing is required, notice must be given as provided here. Failure to receive notice does not invalidate the permit/decision.

### A. Content of notice
Must include: date/time/place; hearing body; general explanation; general description (text or diagram) of property location; and whether a negative declaration or EIR has been prepared and will be considered under CEQA.

### B. Method of notice distribution
As required by Gov. Code §§65090 and 65091:
1. Publish at least once in a newspaper of general circulation at least **15 days** before hearing; and
2. Mail/deliver at least **15 days** before hearing to:
   - a. Owner(s), owner’s agent, applicant
   - b. Each local agency expected to provide essential facilities/services that may be significantly affected
   - c. All property owners within **500 feet** (assessment roll), unless (d) applies
   - d. All property owners within **one-half mile** if property is in any **A** or **R-R** District
   - e. Persons/organizations requesting notice

3. If notice would go to > 1,000 owners (per (a) and (c)), Director of Resource Management may instead place a display ad ≥ 1/8 page in a newspaper at least 15 days before hearing.

### C. Additional notice
Other notice methods may be used. Planning Commission or Zoning Administrator agendas provided to those who requested (in writing) within the calendar year and paid mailing fee set by Board of Supervisors.

### D. Scheduling of hearing
After CEQA documents and staff review/report completion, schedule for hearing on ZA/Planning Commission/Board agenda. Hearing may be continued per (E).

### E. Notice when hearing continued
May continue without new notice so long as continuation does not exceed **35 days** from noticed date. If continued to > 35 days, or date/time not announced at continuation, County must renotice as in (A)-(C).

### F. Notice for waivers
To grant a waiver, notify owners of contiguous property (assessment roll) **10 days** in advance of decision.

## <a id="sec-28-05"></a>28.05 — Compliance with Chapter

Except as otherwise provided:

- **A.** No building erected; no existing building moved/altered/added/enlarged; and no land/building/premises used except among uses permitted in the district where located.
- **B.** No building erected/reconstructed/structurally altered to exceed height limit for the district.
- **C.** No building erected; no existing building altered/enlarged/rebuilt; and no open space encroached/reduced except in conformity with yard/building site area/building location regulations for the district.
- **D.** No yard/open space provided for compliance may be counted as yard/open space for any other building; and no yard/open space on one building site may be counted for a building on another building site.

**History Note:** (Ord. No. 1726, §2)
